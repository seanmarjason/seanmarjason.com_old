What should I be learning?

16 June 2019

When learning new things, one of the big issues I have always faced is prioritising. Figuring out what I should do first, and staying on track when I read that another key skill is required for what I am trying to achieve. Should I try to learn this new skill? Or should I continue on my current path? Do I even know if this new skill is REALLY required? Or is it just someone trying to plug their new tool?

Well, rather than listening to what other people keep saying, I figured data could help me sort through any potential mis-information. So, what is the best source for me to identify skills that will benefit me in the future? Well, how about job descriptions for roles that I would be keen to move into?

But after a quick look on indeed.com I realised that it would take me FAR too long to trawl through all the jobs that are posted, and there would be no way that I could keep up. So, time to automatically scrape data instead!

Note, I am not a programmer, and in no way am suggesting that this is the quickest, most efficient, or most elegant way to structure this. But, it does achieve exactly what I want it to, and so for now its good enough!

    Some key takeways from this:

  • Analysing raw text from job descriptions is not ideal. Looking at the above chart, there is some pollution in the results. For example, I could interpret ‘Excel’ to include both the processing and visualisation components of excel and combine ‘visualisation’ with it, or visualisation could refer to Tableau, or both!
  • I need a way to track when new skills emerge which may not be present in the current research or write ups about these roles.
  • I need a way to generalise this so that I can define a job title, and let this run. Or better yet, get results across many job titles and present them all in one visualisation.

    • But anyway, this gives me what I need right now. Time to brush up on some advanced spreadsheeting …
